Internet Protocol Address is a unique address that is used to identify computers or nodes on the internet.
It is used to locate and find a specific device in the internet in order to request or deliver data.

TCP guarantees the delivery of data to the destination router, has extensive error-checking mechanisms,
provides flow control and acknowledgement of data.
TCP has data sequencing and allows retransmission of lost packets.
UDP is faster, supports broadcasting, is connectionless, capable of delivering data with missing components.
TCP connection is a byte stream. UDP connection is a message stream.
